Analysizing Week 3 Must Start Players

Week 3 Top 3 Must Start Quarterbacks

Number 1: Patrick Mahommes, Kansas City Chiefs

The Chiefs quarterback is on hot start these two weeks, and now he is facing a 49er's defense that has given up 5 touchdowns passes per game and is 25th against the passing yards.
This week Mahommes should be on fire on the field.

Number 2:Ryan Fitzpatrick, Tampa Bay Buccaneers

Fitz "Magic", another quarterback who's on hot start these past two weeks. He will be facing a decent passing defense who they (Steelers), who ranks 15th against the pass.
However, he will be playing at home, which will comfort him to pass the ball.

Number 3: Kirk Cousins, Minnesota Vikings

Cousins has been great since the beginning of the season. He threw 4 touchdowns in week 2, and 2 touchdowns in week 1.
This week, he's playing at home against the Buffalo Bills, a team who has started awful this season.
They have surrendered an average of 6 touchdowns to opposing quarterbacks. Cousins should be a must start

Week 3 Must Start Runningbacks

Number 1: James Conner, Pittsburgh Steelers

Conner has been the "Bell Cow" of the running game since Bell's absence. However, this week he will be playing against the Bucs who has been tight against the run.
Yet, the amount of snaps he will playing and nobody to compete with, his upside will be great this week.

Number 2: Tevin Coleman, Atlanta Falcons

The Falcons will continue to play without Devontae Freeman who's been out with a knee injury, which makes Coleman the primary back for the team.
Though the Saints team is ranked 17th against the run, Coleman's upside is due to mass amount of snaps he will play.

Number 3: Giovani Bernard, Cincinnati Bengals

Nixon, the recent primary back for the Bengals who's out with an injury. Bernard will be taking the primary running back role for Cincinnati.
Bernard should be great to start in PPR format leagues.
